THE ACADEMIC MODEL PROVIDING ACCESS TO HEALTHCARE (AMPATH)

AMPATH is a global partnership of universities and medical centers in North America and Kenya, working together to build smart, holistic, sustainable healthcare systems in Kenya and around the world.

AMPATH leads foundational training and research programs and partners with the Kenyan government to care for a population of 4 million+ people.

We start with access to quality healthcare, and then go beyond to help people thrive through economic empowerment, agricultural training, and self-sufficiency programs.

AMPATH core partners

  1. Moi University in Kenya
  2. Moi Teaching and Referral Hospital in Kenya
  3. AMPATH Consortium of 12+ North American universities and health centers, led by Indiana University

How we make a difference:

  1. CARE

    We provide comprehensive and preventative care through a sustainable health system and programs that build economic independence.

    • HIV + AIDS
    • Cancer
    • Child Health
    • Women’s Health
    • Surgery
    • Economic Empowerment
    • Agriculture
    • Chronic Diseases
  2. TRAINING

    We educate medical students, residents, community health workers, and more so the care system is continuously fed with tomorrow’s health leaders and institutions are mutually strengthened.

    • Kenyan & North American exchange programs
    • Training for medical professionals in Kenya
    • Community health worker education
    • Specialty medical training
  3. RESEARCH

    We conduct research to strengthen care systems, quickly respond to health challenges, and push for breakthroughs in health, policy, and justice worldwide.

    • A network of 20+ institutions collaborating in Africa, North America and Europe
    • $10 Million+ in research funding each year
    • 100+ active research studies
