If you have been diagnosed with HIV, it is only natural to want to know what effect the virus will have on your health in the future and your life-expectancy.

The word used to forecast the likely course of a disease is 'prognosis'.

It comes from an ancient Greek word that means ‘to know beforehand’.

Factors affecting the prognosis of people with HIV

The following factors affect the life-expectancy of people living with HIV:

  1. CD4 cell count. This gives a rough guide to the health of the immune system.
  2. Viral load. The amount of HIV in the blood.
  3. Age.
  4. Developing serious illness because of HIV before HIV treatment is started.
  5. Infection with hepatitis B virus or hepatitis C virus .
  6. Injecting drug use.
  7. Smoking .

Why is prognosis improving?

The simple answer is that potent HIV treatment became available.

The amount of illness and death caused by HIV has fallen dramatically since powerful combinations of anti-HIV drugs became available in 1996.

Doctors have also become skilled at treating the infections that people living with HIV can be vulnerable to when CD4 counts are low.

Looking after your health

There is a lot you can do to look after your health and to give yourself the best chance of living a long and healthy life.

This includes:

  1. Making sure that you go to your HIV clinic for regular check-ups .
  2. Taking your HIV medication and any other treatment properly .
  3. Avoiding infection with hepatitis B or hepatitis C .
  4. Not smoking .
  5. Eating a good, balanced diet.
  6. Exercising regularly.
  7. Looking after your mental health .
  8. Finding support
