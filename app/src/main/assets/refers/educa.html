Who is a Peer?

A peer is someone who belongs to the same social group as another person, meaning that they share at least one important social or demographic characteristic such as age, education, occupation, or risk behavior.

Peer Mentor Means?

  • Understanding what your Peers go through and knowing first hand where it pinches the most. (Living the Experience).
  • It also means being non judgmental no matter the situation.
  • Being a link/ bridge between the peers and the health care providers/service
  • Always using your story or past experience to encourage your fellow peers.
  • Being able to randomly motivate your fellow peers.

What is Peer Mentorship/ Education?

This is the process by which a well-trained and motivated individuals lead an organized educational and skills-building activities with their peers to support and improve young people’s health and well-being.

Activities in peer mentorship

Peer Mentorship activities are participatory and generally take place over an extended period, as opposed to a one-time event. Venues for peer mentorship/ education include Schools, Health centers, parks, bars, social clubs, churches, work settings, and markets—any place where young people spend time together.

  • Qualities should a good Peer Mentor Portray
  • Good listening skills
  • Caring and supportive
  • Confident
  • Reliable and available
  • Willing to invest the time and energy /share experience.
  • Demonstrate empathy but not sympathy
  • Serve as role model and source of trusted information
  • Good social skills, interpersonal and n communication

Effective strategies of conducting Peer mentorship forums/ session.

  • Have Correct and Factual Information
  • Prepare adequate resources for conducting different activities.
  • Always reach out to your peers first.
  • Be creative and SMART

Peer Mentorship can be delivered through??

  • One on one sessions
  • Facilitating group sessions
  • Establishing a connection or rapport with your fellow peers.
  • Organizing Community/Institution forums
  • Health talk sessions
  • Support group discussion

Benefits of peer Mentorship/ education (Adamchak 2006) shows that this approach can:

  • Positively influence young people’s attitudes and knowledge about reproductive health and HIV
  • Influence youth to decrease their number of sexual partners and increase their use of condoms and other contraception
  • Reach groups of youth with education and health materials, such as brochures and condoms
  • Increase the use of sexual and reproductive health services by vulnerable young people
  • Improve community norms about youth and sexual behavior
