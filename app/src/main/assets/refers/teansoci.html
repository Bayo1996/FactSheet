What is VIRAL LOAD?

Viral load is the amount of virus in your blood.

A viral load test is a blood test to count the virus in your blood.

Why is it important?

Knowing your viral load helps your doctor monitor your health and know whether your treatment is working.

How does your treatment work?

When you take your medication everyday, they stop the virus from multiplying so the virus in your body will decrease.

What Does Your Test Results Mean?

  1. High Viral Load.

    There is too much virus in the blood.

    You are not taking your medications properly therefore the treatment is not working properly

    If you take your medication everyday, you can get your viral load become undetectable

  2. Low or Undetectable

    A viral load which is undetectable

    You have very little virus in your blood.

    The drugs you are taking are working well

    A low or undetectable viral load does not mean you no longer have the virus It means it can hardly be measured with the tests we use.

  • *Low or undetectable viral load means you are taking your medicine well and that they are working properly. You are less likely to pass the virus
  • *But If you stop taking your medicines, the viral load will increase.
  • *The chance of passing the virus to others is higher when you have high viral load.
  • *You are also likely to get other infections when your viral load is high.
