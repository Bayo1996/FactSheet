Drug misuse/drug abuse

  • Drugs- chemical agents which are used for cure and prevention of disease
  • Misuse – nonmedical use of drug for wrong purpose
  • Abuse – misused of drug

Common drugs of addicts;

  • Opium/opiates – all narcotics drugs
  • Cannabis/ marijuana
  • Other drugs like diazepam and marungi

The route for their administration; Opium;

  • Central nervous system stimulant with effect similar to amphetamine
  • Administration; smoke, sniff or injection

  • Effect of opium; Excitement, Dilation of pupils, Dizziness and tremors

    High dose cause; Confusion, Seizures, Cardiac and respiratory arrest

    Long term use may cause; Development of psychiatric symptom; paranoid, visual and tactile hallucination

Why People Take Drugs

  • To induce sleep
  • Relive pain, relieve boredom
  • In times of trouble
  • Economic problems
  • Exam failure
  • When bored/idle or to have fun
  • To lessen pain
  • They make you feel good
  • To help one work better
  • For religious reason
  • They help people feel relaxed
  • It’s part of culture

Drugs and substance abuse process


  1. Heroin;
    • Semi synthetic opium, the group includes; Diamorphine and Diacetylmorphine
    • Administration; IV, sniffing and inhalation
    • Effects; powerful euphoria, rapid development of dependence after few days of regular use
  2. Cannabis/marijuana;
    • Administration; smoking leaves, drinking boiled leaves and seeds, chewing leaves
    • Effects; feeling of being important, perceptional distortation, poor judgment and concentration, violent which can be followed by suicidal ideas
  3. Khat/marungi;
    • Administration; oral chewing
    • Effects; at early stage mild euphoria and decreases appetite, at latest phase teeth decay, constipation, depression or paranoid idea,impotence
  4. Tobacco;
    • Derive from leaves of a plant called tabacci nicitano
    • It active ingredient is nicotine which cause addiction
    • Effects; dizziness, increased BP, loss of appetite
    • Administration; smoking, chewing

Presenting compliant of drug abuse;

  • Family may request help before the patient due to irritability or abstinence from home
  • Depressed mood, nervousness or insomnia
  • Direct request for prescription of narcotics or other drugs
  • Request for help to withdraw or stability of their drug use
  • State of intoxication or withdrawal with physical complication of drug use e.g. abscesses or thromboses

Signs of drug withdrawal;

  • Opiod; nausea, sweating and hallucinations
  • Sedatives; anxiety, tremors and hallucination
  • Stimulants; depression and moodiness

Effects of drug abuse on the body; Heroin and opiod;

  • Start with the sense of well being, follow by false perceptions and delusion
  • Cold skin, convulsion and coma due to over dose
  • Slow and shallow breathing or even complete breathing stop
  • Premature delivery, stillbirth and addicted infants
  • Transmission of infection from person to person e.g. hepatitis, HIV and etc through blood contact
  • Bacterial infection on injection sites
  • Drugs depression
  • Loss of libido (interest in sex)
  • Headache and general malaise

Short term effects of marijuana;

  1. Dry mouth and throat
  2. Impair concentration and performance
  3. Increase heart rate which worsen with preexisting heart disease
  4. Decrease social inhibitions
  5. Person present with paranoid, delusion and hallucinations

Long term effects of marijuana;

  1. Decreased testosterone level in men and lower sperm count/infertility
  2. Increase testosterone level in women and increased risk of infertility
  3. Reduce libido
  4. Blocks message going to brain and alters perception, follow by hallucination and delusions
  5. Increased drug intoxication
  6. Risk cancer

Effects of nicotine;

  • Increased heart and breathing rate
  • Dizziness and raise blood pressure
  • Makes body needs more oxygen than normal which lead to heart burden
  • Cause low birth weight and stillbirth

Effect of Miraa

  • Lack of sleep (insomnia)
  • Poor concentration
  • False feeling of well-being
  • Browning of teeth and foul breath
  • Low birth weight, birth defects
  • Impotence
  • Mouth sores and ulcers, tongue
  • Nerve damage leading to numbness
  • Risk of cancers – mouth, lung, throat
  • Depression

Diagnostic features;

  • Physical Injuries while intoxicated, physiological harm/symptom of mental disorder due to drug
  • Harmful social consequences like loss of job, severe family problems or criminality
  • Habitual or harmful chaotic drugs use
  • Difficulty controlling drug use
  • Strong desire to use drugs
  • Tolerance( take heavy dose with no effects)
  • Withdrawal signs, anxiety, tremors etc

Differential diagnosis;

  • Alcohol abuse
  • Symptoms of anxiety or depression may occur with heavy use
  • Depression
  • Generalized anxiety
  • Psychotic disorders
  • Delirium


Information for family and patient;

  • Drug misuses is a chronic, relapsing problem, and controlling or stopping, use often needs several attempts from close relatives and government organs, relapse is always very common
  • Ceasing or reducing drug use will bring psychological, social and physical benefits
  • Using some drugs during pregnancy risks harming the baby
  • For IV drugs users there is high risk of infection with diseases of blood contact

Medication;

  • Don’t give any medication
  • Convince patient to see specialist

    • Referral; all case of drug abuse should be referred to the specialist
